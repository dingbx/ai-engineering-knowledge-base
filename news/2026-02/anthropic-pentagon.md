# Anthropic-Pentagon Conflict

**Date:** 2026-02-28
**Category:** Policy

---

## Summary

The Trump administration cut off Anthropic after the AI startup refused to remove guardrails preventing military use of Claude for mass surveillance and autonomous weapons. Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth called Anthropic a "supply-chain risk."

---

## Timeline

1. Anthropic drew "red lines" for military use
2. Pentagon requested removal of restrictions
3. Anthropic refused
4. Hegseth designated Anthropic as supply-chain risk
5. Other contractors barred from using Anthropic AI for military work

---

## Anthropic's Position

CEO Dario Amodei:
> "We are still interested in working with them as long as it is in line with our red lines."

> "We believe that crossing those lines is contrary to American values."

Red lines:
- No mass surveillance on Americans
- No autonomous weapons
- 98-99% of other use cases acceptable

---

## Impact

Amodei's assessment:
> "The impact of this designation is fairly small."

Company remains valued at $380B with 2,500 employees.

---

## Context

Meanwhile, xAI signed deal to allow Grok use in classified military systems, taking opposite approach.
